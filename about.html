     Hello and thanks for visiting! At this point in time, if you've arrived on my page, you are likely either another student in my Island Foxes cohort at Dev Bootcamp, an instructor there, or maybe someone considering encrolling at DBC. If you are the latter, feel free to email me for any questions and I'd be happy to answer them as best I can, as other DBC alums did for me.

     As a woman in my early 30's, I feel like I've lived many different lives before arriving here. In college, I majored in Theatre & Dance, and moved to New York City to pursue acting. Throughout the years, acting evolved into improv, which evolved into stand-up comedy, my main love. Somewhere around mid-2013, I started getting tired of performing stand-up. I felt my life had changed so much in the past few years that the jokes I had taken so long to write and fine tune, were no longer who I was, and the idea of starting again with a brand new set seemed too overwhelming to begin without first taking a break. So on New Year's eve of 2014, I boarded a plane and set off for a two month backpacking adventure through Thailand and Australia, which changed everything for me.

     The trip was my first solo travel experience, and though I met up with a couple friends in Thailand for certain legs of the trip, it was mostly just me by my lonesome. In Khao Lak, I did a live aboard boat trip to the Similan Islands and got my PADI open water SCUBA certification, and fell immediately in love with the sport, and with travel in general. As you probably know, SCUBA isn't exactly an affordable activity for the average person, so it made me really start to consider other careers that would provide a more comfortable income. Engineering was always something I wanted to learn but hadn't really explored it yet. Later in 2014, I took an HTML/CSS workshop, followed by an Intro to Javascript class, and a MYSQL course. I was initially intrigued by the idea of getting paid well to be creative and build things, but I had no idea how much I'd love it just on a day to day basis. Writing programs makes me feel like I'm playing a game or solving a puzzle, and I often find myself staying up late and coding for fun rather than watching Netflix.

     I guess it's a DBC tradition that I have to tell you something quirky about me. For one, I'm obessed, and this cannot be understated, with Great White Sharks. I've been reading about them and watching videos about them from as young as I can remember being, and on my trip to Australia, had the incredible opportunity to volunteer on a cage dive ecotourist operation run by the legendary, Rodney Fox, and his son Andrew, who is an exceptionally talented photographer and very brave human.

     Also, I always try to think of myself as 2 years older than I actually am, so that when I turn that age, I won't feel so old because I will have already gotten used to it. :)
